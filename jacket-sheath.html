JACKET / SHEATH

PVC, PE, LSZH, TPU, Neoprene, Silicone — outer sheath materials for every installation environment.

// OVERVIEW
WHAT IS THE CABLE JACKET?

The outer jacket (or sheath) is the cable's final protective layer. It provides mechanical protection against abrasion, impact, and crushing; environmental protection against moisture, chemicals, UV radiation, and temperature extremes; and electrical safety by preventing accidental contact with the cable's internal components.

The jacket material is selected based on the installation environment, operating temperature, required flame rating, and chemical exposure the cable will encounter throughout its service life.

// SELECTION
ENVIRONMENT SELECTION GUIDE
Indoor Fixed Installation
PVC is standard for most indoor fixed wiring. LSZH required in occupied buildings, transport, and any enclosed space where smoke in a fire would be dangerous. Check local fire regulations — PVC is banned in many countries for these applications.
Direct Burial / Underground
Use PE or HDPE jacket over armoured cable. PE resists moisture migration and soil chemicals over decades of buried service. Never bury PVC-jacketed cables without additional duct protection — PVC degrades from soil chemicals and root penetration.
Industrial / Moving Applications
PUR (Polyurethane) for drag chains, robot arms, and any application where the cable is continuously flexed and subject to oil splash or abrasion. PVC will crack and fail in continuous flex applications. PUR can withstand millions of flex cycles.
