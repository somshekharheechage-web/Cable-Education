JACKET / SHEATH

PVC, PE, LSZH, TPU, neoprene, silicone — outer jacket materials, their properties, and installation environment selection guide.

// OVERVIEW

THE ROLE OF THE OUTER JACKET

The outer jacket (or sheath) is the cable's last line of defence against the installation environment. It protects all internal components from mechanical damage, moisture ingress, UV radiation, chemical attack, and temperature extremes. It also carries the cable's identification marking and colour coding.

The jacket does not carry current or signal — its role is purely protective. But a jacket failure allows moisture into the core, which can cause insulation degradation, short circuits, and corrosion of conductors over time. Jacket selection is therefore as critical as any other design decision.

// MATERIALS

JACKET MATERIALS

🔵
PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)
Max 70°C. Low cost, flexible, inherently flame retardant. Available in dozens of colours. Good moisture and abrasion resistance. Releases HCl smoke in fire — avoid in enclosed spaces. The default choice for general-purpose cables in moderate environments.
⬜
Polyethylene (PE / MDPE / HDPE)
Max 80°C. Excellent moisture and chemical resistance. Semi-rigid — provides good mechanical protection. UV-stabilised grades for outdoor use. Standard for direct burial, underground ducts, and aerial cables. Black PE is the most UV-resistant colour.
🟢
LSZH (Low Smoke Zero Halogen)
Max 70°C. Minimal toxic smoke and no halogen acid gas when burning. Mandatory in tunnels, public buildings, rail, data centres, and offshore platforms. Less flexible than PVC and slightly more expensive. Must meet IEC 61034 (smoke) and IEC 60754 (halogen) tests.
🟡
TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane)
Max 80°C. Outstanding abrasion resistance — 10× better than PVC. Excellent oil and chemical resistance. Used in robotics, drag chains, food processing, and any cable subject to constant mechanical wear. More expensive than PVC but dramatically outlasts it in abrasive environments.
🔴
Neoprene (CR — Chloroprene Rubber)
Max 90°C. Excellent weather, ozone, and oil resistance. Good mechanical toughness. Used in mining cables, welding leads, portable power tools, and marine applications where rubber flexibility and oil resistance are both required.
🟠
Silicone Rubber
Max 180°C continuously. Remains flexible at −60°C. Used for furnace and kiln wiring, aerospace, medical equipment, and high-temperature industrial applications. Fragile surface — not suitable where abrasion is a concern without an overbraid.

// SELECTION GUIDE

CHOOSING THE RIGHT JACKET

Installation EnvironmentRecommended Jacket
Indoor office / commercial buildingPVC or LSZH
Public building, tunnel, metro, railLSZH (mandatory)
Direct burial / undergroundMDPE or HDPE (black)
Outdoor aerial (UV exposure)UV-stabilised PE or black PVC
Industrial factory floorPUR or Neoprene (oil/abrasion)
Robot arm / drag chainTPU or PUR (flex + abrasion)
High temperature (>90°C)Silicone or PTFE jacketed
Marine / offshoreLSZH or Neoprene
Mining / quarryNeoprene or EPR jacketed
