// TUTORIAL — CABLE COMPONENTS

FILLERS & FILLING COMPOUNDS

PP yarn, PE rods, swellable tape, jelly compounds — maintaining cable roundness, stability, and water protection.

// OVERVIEW
WHAT ARE FILLERS?

Within the cable structure, fillers or filling compounds are used to maintain the shape and structure of the cable, reduce stress on the conductors, provide additional insulation support, and increase cable service life.

The specific type of filler depends on the cable type, application, environmental conditions, and specific performance requirements. Manufacturers customise filler selection based on the needs of each cable design.

// FILLER TYPES
TYPES OF FILLERS
🧵
Polypropylene (PP) Yarn
The most common dry filler. Non-hygroscopic, lightweight, and compatible with all common insulation materials. Occupies the interstices between cores to maintain the round cable form during armour and jacket application.
⬜
Polyethylene (PE) Rods
Solid PE rods used in power cables and larger multi-core constructions. Provide dimensional stability and consistent round form. Compatible with PE and XLPE bedding extrusion. Used where yarn would compress excessively under armour pressure.
💧
Swellable (Water-Blocking) Tape
Tape containing superabsorbent polymer powder that swells to many times its original volume when wetted. Applied between cores and as a longitudinal barrier. Required for direct burial and submarine cables — prevents water propagation along the cable length in the event of jacket damage.
🫙
Petroleum Jelly / Gel Compounds
Semi-solid filling compound injected into the interstices of the cable during manufacturing. Provides excellent longitudinal and radial water blocking without the weight of swellable materials. Common in direct-burial telephone and data cables (filled cables). Messy to terminate — special cleaning solvents required.
🔴
LSZH Fillers
Non-hygroscopic filling compound designed for use in LSZH cables where the entire cable construction must meet halogen-free requirements. Standard PP yarn contains no halogens, but some gel compounds may — verify compound composition against LSZH standards.
// KEY RULE
THE ROUNDNESS RULE
Why Roundness Matters
A round cable cross-section is not just aesthetic — it is functional. Armour wires or tapes applied over an oval or irregular assembly will have unequal contact pressure: tighter on one side, loose on the other. This causes uneven mechanical protection, potential core damage under crush loads, and difficulties achieving uniform jacket wall thickness. Fillers are the primary tool for achieving and maintaining cable roundness throughout the manufacturing process.
