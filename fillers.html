FILLERS

PP yarn, PE rods, water-blocking tapes, and flooding compounds — how fillers create a round, stable multi-core cable.

// OVERVIEW

WHAT ARE CABLE FILLERS?

Fillers occupy the interstitial gaps between round cable cores when they are assembled into a multi-core construction. Without fillers, the spaces between cores create voids in the cable cross-section — resulting in an oval or irregular shape that makes armour application difficult and allows the cores to shift position under mechanical stress.

Good filler design achieves a circular, compact, stable cable cross-section while adding minimal weight and cost. Fillers also play a role in water-blocking, fire performance, and — in some designs — cushioning the cores against armour wire penetration.

// FILLER TYPES

TYPES OF FILLERS

🧵
Polypropylene (PP) Yarn
The most common filler. Lightweight, low cost, chemically inert, and thermally stable to 130°C. Easily coloured. Applied by wrapping bundles around and between cores. Available in different weights (tex) to fill different gap sizes. Standard for power, control, and instrumentation cables.
🔵
PE / PP Rods and Profiles
Extruded solid or shaped profiles that fit precisely into the interstices between specific core configurations — especially triangular gaps in 3-core and 4-core cables. Provide a very consistent, dimensionally stable cross-section. Used where precise roundness is critical for subsequent processing.
💧
Water-Blocking (Swellable) Tape / Yarn
Filled with superabsorbent polymer (SAP) particles that swell rapidly on contact with water, sealing any longitudinal water migration path along the cable. Essential for direct burial, submarine, and any cable where water ingress at a cut end or damaged section must not travel along the cable length.
🟡
Jelly-Filled / Flooding Compound
Petroleum or synthetic gel that fills all voids in the cable core under positive pressure. Completely blocks water and moisture migration. Standard in outdoor and direct-burial telephone cables, some data cables, and submarine cables. Messy to work with — gloves and solvents required for termination.
🔬
LSZH / Flame Retardant Fillers
PP or PE fillers compounded with flame retardant additives or LSZH compounds. Used in cables where the filler contributes to the overall fire performance rating. Important in cables for transit, offshore, and public building applications where the entire cable construction must meet fire standards.
📰
Binder / Wrapping Tape
Polyester, nylon, or paper tapes applied over the filled core assembly to bind everything together before bedding or armour. Not a filler in the traditional sense but performs the same function of maintaining cable geometry. Overlap of 10–25% is typical.

// DESIGN RULE

WHY ROUNDNESS MATTERS

💡 The roundness rule
A cable must present a circular cross-section to the armoring machine. An oval assembly causes armour wires to apply unequal pressure around the circumference — some wires lift off the surface while others embed into the bedding. This causes armour gaps, bedding damage, and potential damage to the cores underneath. Always verify roundness with a go/no-go gauge after laying up before proceeding to armoring.
