// OVERVIEW

WHAT ARE ELECTRICAL CABLES?

Electrical cables are designed to carry electrical current for power distribution, motor control, lighting circuits, and industrial automation. Unlike communication cables — which prioritise signal integrity — electrical cables are engineered for current-carrying capacity, voltage withstand, and thermal performance.

Selection depends on voltage rating, current load, ambient temperature, installation environment (indoor, outdoor, underground, submerged), and applicable standards such as IEC 60502, BS 7671, or NEC.

// CABLE FAMILIES

TYPES OF ELECTRICAL CABLE

⚡
Low Voltage Power Cable (≤1 kV)
Used for general power distribution in buildings, factories, and infrastructure. Typically XLPE or PVC insulated with PVC or LSZH jacket. Examples: NYY, NYM, SWA armoured cable.
🔆
Medium Voltage Cable (1 kV–36 kV)
For underground distribution networks, industrial feeders, and substations. XLPE insulation with semi-conductive screens, metal screen, and armour. Designed per IEC 60502-2.
🏭
Control & Instrumentation Cable
Multi-core cables for carrying control signals, 4–20 mA loops, and relay circuits. Individual or overall screened to reject interference in industrial environments.
🌡️
Heat-Resistant Cable
Silicone or PTFE insulated for use near furnaces, boilers, and engines where ambient temperatures exceed the limits of standard PVC (70°C) or XLPE (90°C).
💧
Direct Burial / Submarine Cable
Polyethylene outer sheath and steel wire or tape armour. Designed to resist soil pressure, moisture ingress, and mechanical damage without conduit protection.
🔥
Fire-Resistant Cable (FRC)
Maintains circuit integrity during a fire. Uses mica tape over conductors. Required for emergency lighting, fire alarms, and safety system circuits per IEC 60331.

// CONDUCTOR SIZING

CONDUCTOR SIZING GUIDE

Conductor cross-section determines current-carrying capacity (ampacity). Under-sizing causes overheating; over-sizing wastes material. Always consult IEC 60364 or local wiring regulations for certified sizing tables.

Cross-section (mm²)Approx. Ampacity (in conduit)Approx. Ampacity (in air)Typical Application
1.5 mm²13 A17 ALighting circuits
2.5 mm²18 A23 ASocket outlets, small appliances
4 mm²24 A30 AShower units, cookers
6 mm²31 A38 ASub-main, large appliances
10 mm²43 A52 ADistribution boards
16 mm²57 A70 AMain incomer, motor feeders
25 mm²75 A92 ALarge motor feeders
50 mm²105 A133 ALV switchboard feeders
95 mm²153 A196 AMain supply cables
185 mm²217 A278 AHV/LV transformer connections
⚠ Derating Factors
Ampacity values must be derated for ambient temperature above 30°C, grouping of cables in bundles, and installation in thermal insulation. Always apply correction factors from the relevant standard before finalising conductor size.
