STRENGTH MEMBERS

Aramid, fibreglass, steel wire, UHMWPE — tensile strength members for aerial, submarine, and demanding cable installations.

// OVERVIEW
WHAT ARE STRENGTH MEMBERS?

In certain cables — especially those for outdoor, aerial, or submarine installations — strength members are incorporated to enhance tensile strength and support the weight and tension forces the cable must withstand during installation and service.

Glass fiber itself is brittle and copper has limited tensile strength. Without dedicated strength members, pulling tension during installation, or the dead weight of a suspended cable, would stretch or snap the conductive elements. The choice of strength member depends on the required tensile strength, environmental conditions, weight budget, and conductivity requirements.

// TYPES
STRENGTH MEMBER TYPES
🟡
Aramid Yarns (Kevlar)
High-strength synthetic fibers with exceptional tensile strength (up to 3.6 GPa), near-zero thermal expansion, and light weight. Non-conductive. The standard strength member in fiber optic cables and portable power cables. Provide structural integrity without adding significant weight or diameter.
⚪
Fibreglass (GRP) Rods
Glass fibre-reinforced plastic central member. High tensile strength, low weight, non-conductive, and dimensionally stable. Common central strength member in loose-tube fiber optic cables. Also used in self-supporting aerial cables where a non-conductive element is required for safety.
⬛
Steel Wire
Used as strength members in cables requiring very high tensile load capacity — submarine cables, aerial power lines, and overhead self-supporting data cables. Galvanised steel wires carry enormous pulling tension during installation and support the cable's dead weight in aerial spans. Conductive — requires careful isolation from signal conductors.
🔵
Synthetic Fabrics (Nylon / Polyester)
Woven or braided fabric tapes or yarn used in low-voltage and communication cables for moderate tensile strength. Lightweight, flexible, and resistant to moisture and UV. Common in portable and data centre cables where aramid cost is not justified by the load requirements.
🟢
UHMWPE (Dyneema)
Ultra-high molecular weight polyethylene — one of the strongest fibres available for its weight (15× stronger than steel by weight). Used in deep-sea ROV cables, military cables, and extreme-flex applications where weight and strength are both critical. Very low creep and excellent fatigue resistance.
