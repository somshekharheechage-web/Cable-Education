EXTRUDER MACHINE

Melting polymer compound and forcing it over a moving conductor — applying insulation, bedding, and jacket layers to a cable.

// OVERVIEW
THE EXTRUSION PROCESS

A cable extruder heats thermoplastic or thermoset polymer (PVC, XLPE, PE, LSZH, silicone) and forces it through a crosshead die over the moving conductor. As the material exits and cools in a water trough, it solidifies into a uniform insulation or jacket layer.

Extrusion is used at multiple stages: insulation of individual conductors, bedding between cores and armour, and jacketing the outer sheath. Each pass requires precise control of temperature, screw speed, line speed, and die geometry.

// COMPONENTS
KEY EXTRUDER COMPONENTS
1
Hopper
Material feed point for polymer granules or pellets. A hopper dryer removes moisture from hygroscopic materials (PVC, LSZH) — moisture in the melt causes bubbles, voids, and poor surface finish.
2
Barrel & Screw
Heated cylinder containing a rotating screw. Three zones: Feed (conveys granules), Compression (melts and compresses), and Metering (delivers uniform melt to the die).
3
Temperature Zones
3–6 independent heating zones controlled by PID controllers. Temperature rises from feed zone (~140°C for PVC) to metering zone (~190°C). Crosshead and die also have independent heating zones.
4
Crosshead Die
Redirects melt 90° onto the moving conductor. Contains a die tip (conductor passes through) and a die (shapes outer surface). The gap determines wall thickness and concentricity.
5
Water Trough & Cooling
Cable passes into a water cooling trough (typically 20–60 m). Water staged warm-to-cold to prevent surface crazing. For XLPE, a steam or nitrogen CV tube is used instead.
6
Spark Tester
Inline high-voltage electrode (5–30 kV) tests every centimetre of insulation for pinholes and voids. Any fault triggers an alarm. Mandatory per IEC 60811.
// DIE TYPES
PRESSURE vs TUBE EXTRUSION
Pressure Extrusion
Die tip is flush with the die orifice. Melt contacts conductor under pressure inside the die — excellent concentricity and adhesion. Used for PVC, LSZH, and PE insulation.
Tube (Vacuum) Extrusion
Die tip protrudes beyond the die. A tube of melt forms and is drawn down onto the conductor outside the die. A vacuum port collapses the tube. Used for XLPE where insulation must not bond to conductor screen.
// CONTROL
