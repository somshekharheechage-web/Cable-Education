AUTOMOTIVE CABLES

Wire harnesses, high-voltage EV cables, CAN bus, LIN, and sensor leads — cables for the harshest mobile environments.

// OVERVIEW
AUTOMOTIVE CABLE CHALLENGES

Automotive cables must withstand extreme temperature swings (−40°C to +150°C under the hood), continuous vibration, exposure to oil, fuel, coolant, UV radiation, and abrasion — while remaining as light and compact as possible.

A modern passenger vehicle contains 1,500–3,000 individual wires bundled into harnesses that can stretch over 4 km total. The shift to electric vehicles has dramatically increased electrical content per vehicle.

// ELECTRIC VEHICLES
HIGH VOLTAGE EV CABLES
🔋
Battery Pack Cables
Large cross-section (35–150 mm²) orange cables at 400–800 V DC. Silicone or XLPE insulation. Shielded against EMI from switching currents. Orange colour mandatory per ISO 6469-3.
⚡
Motor Drive Cables
Three-phase AC from inverter to motor. Must handle high-frequency PWM switching noise. Shielded, routed away from LV signal wiring.
🔌
DC Fast Charge Cables
Liquid-cooled cables for 150–350 kW fast charging. Cooling allows smaller conductors despite very high current — keeps the cable flexible for customer use.
// DATA BUSES
AUTOMOTIVE DATA BUS CABLES
1
CAN Bus (Controller Area Network)
Twisted pair, 120 Ω impedance. The dominant vehicle network for engine management, ABS, airbags, and body electronics. CAN FD extends speed to 8 Mbps.
2
LIN Bus (Local Interconnect Network)
Single-wire, 19.2 kbps. Low-cost for non-critical functions: seat adjustment, mirror control, climate modules. Uses vehicle chassis as the return path.
3
MOST Bus (Media Oriented Systems Transport)
Plastic optical fiber (POF) ring for infotainment, audio, and video. Immune to powertrain EMI and audio amplifier interference.
4
Automotive Ethernet (100BASE-T1 / 1000BASE-T1)
Single unshielded twisted pair for ADAS cameras, radar, LiDAR, and domain controllers. 100 Mbps or 1 Gbps — dramatically lighter than 4-pair standard Ethernet.
