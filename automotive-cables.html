// OVERVIEW

AUTOMOTIVE CABLE CHALLENGES

Automotive cables operate in one of the harshest environments any cable faces. They must withstand extreme temperature swings (−40°C to +150°C under the hood), continuous vibration, exposure to oil, fuel, coolant, and brake fluid, UV radiation, and abrasion — all while remaining as light and compact as possible to meet vehicle weight and packaging targets.

The global shift to electric vehicles (EVs) and advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) has dramatically increased the electrical content per vehicle, making cable engineering a critical discipline in modern automotive design.

// WIRE HARNESS

PRIMARY WIRE & HARNESSES

A modern passenger vehicle contains 1,500–3,000 individual wires bundled into harnesses that can stretch over 4 km in total length. Each wire in the harness is a primary automotive wire — a single conductor with a thin cross-linked insulation jacket colour-coded per OEM or ISO 6722 standards.

// ELECTRIC VEHICLES

HIGH VOLTAGE EV CABLES

Battery electric vehicles (BEVs) and plug-in hybrids (PHEVs) require a completely separate high-voltage (HV) wiring system operating at 400 V or 800 V DC. These cables must be unmistakably identified (orange insulation per ISO 6469-3), heavily shielded against EMI, and designed to prevent accidental contact.

🔋
Battery Pack Cables
Large cross-section (35–150 mm²) orange cables connecting battery cells to the main junction box. Silicone or XLPE insulation rated to 600 V DC. Shielded to suppress EMI from switching currents.
⚡
Motor Drive Cables
Three-phase AC cables from inverter to motor. Must handle high-frequency switching noise from the inverter PWM signal — requires shielded construction and careful routing away from LV signal wiring.
🔌
DC Fast Charge Cables
Liquid-cooled cables for 150–350 kW DC fast charging stations. Cooling allows smaller conductor cross-sections despite very high current, keeping the cable flexible for customer use.
🌡️
Thermal Management
HV cables include temperature sensors to monitor conductor heat. Orange outer jacket is mandatory by ISO standard. Bright orange colour alerts emergency responders and service technicians to shock hazard.

// DATA BUSES

AUTOMOTIVE DATA BUS CABLES

1
CAN Bus (Controller Area Network)
Twisted pair, 120 Ω impedance. The dominant vehicle network for engine management, ABS, airbags, and body electronics. CAN FD extends speed to 8 Mbps. The pair twist rate is critical — untwisting at connectors degrades signal integrity.
2
LIN Bus (Local Interconnect Network)
Single-wire, 19.2 kbps. Low-cost network for non-critical functions: seat adjustment, mirror control, climate system, door modules. Uses the vehicle chassis as the return path — simplifies wiring but limits speed and distance.
3
MOST Bus (Media Oriented Systems Transport)
Fiber optic ring network for infotainment, audio, and video. Plastic optical fiber (POF) runs in a ring topology throughout the vehicle cabin. Immune to EMI from the powertrain and audio amplifiers.
4
Automotive Ethernet (100BASE-T1 / 1000BASE-T1)
Single unshielded twisted pair for ADAS cameras, radar, LiDAR, and domain controllers. 100 Mbps or 1 Gbps over a single pair with advanced DSP — reduces harness weight compared to four-pair standard Ethernet.
