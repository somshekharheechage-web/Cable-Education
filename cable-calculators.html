📐 K Factor Method

Assembly OD Formula
Assembly OD = Core OD × K + 2 × Filler
Standard K factors for circular core arrangement. Filler addition increases the effective overall diameter on both sides.
K Factor Reference Table
2c=2.000 · 3c=2.155 · 4c=2.414 · 5c=2.701 · 7c=3.000
Based on geometric packing of equal-diameter circles in a circular arrangement. Derived from tangent circle geometry.
OD with Binder Tape
Final OD = Assembly OD + 2 × Tape Thickness
Binder tape wraps the assembly and adds to diameter on both sides.
💡 Engineering Tip
The K factor method gives a theoretical minimum OD. In practice, add 2–5% tolerance for lay-up variation, especially with odd-count cores or non-circular cross-sections.

⚡ IEC 60228 Formulas

DC Resistance at 20°C
R₂₀ = ρ / A × 1000
ρ = resistivity (Ω·mm²/m), A = cross-section area (mm²). Result in Ω/km.
Temperature Correction
R_T = R₂₀ × [1 + α(T − 20)]
α = temperature coefficient. Annealed copper: 0.00393/°C · Aluminium: 0.00403/°C · Hard drawn Cu: 0.00381/°C
Total Resistance
R_total = R_T × Length / 1000
Total resistance of the conductor for a given length in Ω.
📋 IEC 60228 Reference Values
IEC 60228 specifies maximum DC resistance limits at 20°C for conductors up to 2500 mm². Always verify against the standard tables for compliance.

🌀 Braid Coverage Formulas

Braid Coverage %
C = 1 − (1 − n·d / (π·D·sinθ))²
n = carriers per group, d = wire Ø (mm), D = cable OD (mm), θ = braid angle. Multiply by 100 for %. Capped at 100%.
Braid Pitch
Pitch = π × D / tan(θ)
Axial distance (mm) for one complete revolution of a braid strand around the cable.
Total Wire Count
Total Wires = n × 2
Two groups of n carriers each (S-twist and Z-twist) interweave to form the braid pattern.
Braid OD
Braid OD ≈ Cable OD + 2 × wire Ø
Approximate outer diameter of cable after braiding is applied.
✅ Coverage Guidelines
≥ 90% — Excellent EMI shielding · 70–90% — Standard shielding · < 70% — Limited protection
