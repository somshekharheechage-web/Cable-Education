JACKETING MACHINE

The final extrusion pass — applying the outer protective sheath that gives the cable its identity, colour, markings, and protection against the installation environment.

// OVERVIEW

WHAT IS JACKETING?

Jacketing is the final step in cable manufacturing. The outer jacket — also called the outer sheath — is extruded over the armour or directly over the laid-up cores to provide the cable's last line of defence: moisture, UV radiation, chemical exposure, abrasion, and mechanical damage.

The jacket also carries the cable's permanent marking — manufacturer name, voltage rating, standard compliance, and sequential metre marks. It is the cable's identity and primary weatherproof barrier.

// PROCESS

JACKETING LINE PROCESS

1
Pay-off & Pre-heat
The armoured cable is fed from a drum or accumulator. A pre-heater warms the cable surface to improve adhesion of the jacket compound — especially important for PE and LSZH over steel armour where adhesion failures would allow water ingress under the jacket.
2
Extrusion Crosshead
Identical in principle to the insulation extruder. Die size is set to give the target jacket wall thickness per the cable specification. Pressure extrusion is standard. Wall thickness eccentricity must not exceed ±10% of nominal per IEC 60502.
3
Marking
An ink-jet printer or embossing wheel applies sequential metre marks, cable designation, voltage rating, manufacturer name, and standard compliance. Marking must be legible and permanent — tested by rubbing with a damp cloth per IEC 60811-1-3.
4
Cooling Trough
The jacketed cable passes through a long water cooling trough — staged warm-to-cold to prevent surface crazing. For large cables, cooling troughs can exceed 100 m to achieve full solidification at high line speeds before the take-up drum.
5
Final Testing & Drum Take-up
The finished cable passes through an OD laser gauge, then is wound onto a shipping drum. A sample from each drum is taken for routine electrical (insulation resistance, voltage test) and mechanical (tensile, elongation, wall thickness) testing before release.

// QUALITY TESTS

JACKET QUALITY CHECKS

📏
Wall Thickness
Measured at 6 points around the cross-section. Minimum wall must meet or exceed the cable standard. Eccentricity = (max − min) / max × 100%. Max eccentricity typically limited to 10–15%.
🔥
Flame Retardancy
IEC 60332-1 (single cable) or IEC 60332-3 (cable bundles). The jacket must self-extinguish after removing the flame. LSZH also tested for smoke density (IEC 61034) and halogen acid gas (IEC 60754).
💪
Tensile & Elongation
Dumbbell specimens pulled to failure per IEC 60811. Minimum tensile strength and elongation at break must be met before and after thermal ageing at 100°C for 7 days.
🌡️
Cold Bend & Impact
Cable bent around a mandrel or struck at −15°C to −40°C. No jacket cracking permitted. Critical for cables installed in cold climates, refrigerated stores, or outdoor winter environments.
