CONDUCTORS

Copper, aluminium, CCA, CCS, and silver-plated conductors — materials, IEC 60228 classes, and selection guidance.

// OVERVIEW

WHAT ARE CONDUCTORS?

Conductors are the core elements of a cable through which electrical current flows. They are typically made of copper or aluminium due to their high conductivity, and may be a single solid wire or a stranded assembly of multiple smaller wires that enhance flexibility and durability.

Conductor selection directly impacts a cable's resistance, weight, cost, and flexibility. The choice of material, cross-section, and stranding class must match the electrical load, installation conditions, and applicable standard — typically IEC 60228.

// CONDUCTOR MATERIALS

CONDUCTOR TYPES

🟠
Copper (Annealed)
The industry standard. Resistivity 0.01724 Ω·mm²/m at 20°C. Excellent conductivity, flexibility, and solderability. Used in virtually all power, data, and signal cables. More expensive than aluminium but offers superior performance in all classes.
⬜
Aluminium
Resistivity 0.0282 Ω·mm²/m — about 61% of copper's conductivity. Lighter and significantly cheaper. Used in overhead power lines, large underground feeders, and building wire where weight and cost are priorities. Requires larger cross-section for equivalent resistance.
🔵
Copper-Clad Aluminium (CCA)
Aluminium core bonded with a thin copper layer. Lighter and cheaper than solid copper. Acceptable for fixed wiring but not for flexing applications — the copper layer can crack with repeated bending. Must not be used where solid copper is specified.
⚫
Copper-Clad Steel (CCS)
Steel core with copper coating. High tensile strength for overhead and messenger wire applications. Lower conductivity than pure copper. The skin effect at RF frequencies means current flows mainly in the copper layer — effective for antenna and CATV drop wire.
⚪
Silver-Plated Copper
Copper conductor with thin silver coating. Silver has the highest conductivity of any metal (0.0159 Ω·mm²/m). Plating reduces oxidation and improves high-frequency performance. Used in aerospace, military, microwave, and precision signal cables.
🟡
Tinned Copper
Copper coated with a thin tin layer. Prevents oxidation in harsh or humid environments. Easier to solder than bare copper. Standard for marine cables, automotive wiring, and connectors exposed to moisture or corrosive atmospheres.

// IEC 60228 CLASSES

CONDUCTOR CLASSES

IEC 60228 defines conductor classes based on flexibility and construction. The class determines the maximum number and minimum diameter of individual strands.

ClassConstructionFlexibilityTypical Application
Class 1Solid single wireRigid — fixed installation onlyBuilding wiring, conduit installation
Class 2Stranded, minimum number of wiresLow — fixed installationPower cables, distribution cables
Class 5Flexible — many fine wiresHigh — portable useFlexible cords, portable tools, extension leads
Class 6Extra-flexible — very fine wiresVery high — continuous flexingWelding cables, drag chains, robotics
