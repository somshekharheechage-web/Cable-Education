ETHERNET CABLES

Cat5e to Cat8 twisted-pair networking — construction, categories, shielding, and installation.

// OVERVIEW
WHAT ARE ETHERNET CABLES?

Ethernet cables are the standard medium for wired local area networks (LAN). They transmit data between computers, routers, switches, and servers using four twisted copper wire pairs.

Unlike wireless, Ethernet provides consistent bandwidth, lower latency, and greater security — making it the preferred choice for fixed installations in offices, data centres, and industrial environments.

// CONSTRUCTION
CABLE CONSTRUCTION

Conductors

4 twisted pairs (8 wires total) of copper. Solid conductors for fixed installations, stranded for patch cables. Each pair is colour-coded and twisted at a different rate to reduce crosstalk.

Insulation

Each conductor is insulated with polyethylene (PE) or FEP. The dielectric constant affects signal speed (velocity of propagation) and must be consistent along the entire cable length.

Shielding

UTP cables rely on twisting alone. Shielded types add aluminium foil (F/) around pairs or braided copper (S/) around all pairs — essential near motors, power cables, or in industrial environments.

Connectors

All Ethernet cables terminate in RJ45 connectors with 8 gold-plated pins. Wiring follows T568A or T568B — these must match at both ends for a straight-through cable.

// SHIELDING
SHIELDING TYPES
🔵
UTP — Unshielded Twisted Pair
No overall shield. Noise rejection by pair twisting only. Lightest, most flexible, easiest to terminate. Fine for most office and home environments.
🟡
F/UTP — Foil over all pairs
Single aluminium foil wraps all four pairs together. Good external EMI protection without bulk of braided shielding. Standard in Cat 6a.
🟠
S/FTP — Braid + individual foils
Each pair has individual foil, plus outer braided screen. Maximum EMI protection. Used in Cat 7 and Cat 8 for demanding environments.
// INSTALLATION
INSTALLATION GUIDELINES
1
Keep runs under 100 m
IEEE 802.3 limits horizontal cable runs to 100 m for Cat 5e through Cat 6a. Use a switch or media converter for longer distances.
2
Avoid sharp bends
Minimum bend radius = 4× cable diameter. Sharp bends deform twisted pairs, increase crosstalk, and degrade performance permanently.
3
Separate from power cables
Keep at least 200 mm away from power cables. Where cables must cross, do so at 90° to minimise inductive coupling.
4
Match components to the same category
A link performs to its lowest-rated component. Cat 6a cable with Cat 5e patch panels = Cat 5e performance.
5
Test after installation
Certify with a calibrated cable tester — verify wire map, length, attenuation, NEXT, and return loss. Required for warranty.
